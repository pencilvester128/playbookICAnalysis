That makes the entire brand feel more thoughtful than a typical multi-service advisory site. The message is not “we do a lot of things.” The message is “we solve a very specific type of wealth problem in several related contexts.” That is a real strategic advantage.

Main opportunity

The next move is to turn your free tools into a more intentional lead magnet system

Right now, your site does an excellent job educating and building trust. The opportunity is to make that education lead somewhere more clearly. In other words, your tools should not only help visitors; they should also help you identify who those visitors are, what problem they care about, and what kind of follow-up will feel most relevant.

Sample guide copy

The Concentrated Stock Playbook

A clear, practical guide for people who know they need to diversify but do not want to trigger a major tax mistake.

If a large portion of your net worth is tied up in one stock, you already know the problem. The risk is real, but so is the tax bill. That tension is what keeps most people stuck.

This guide walks through the actual options in plain English — how to protect the downside, how to think about taxes, and how strategies like collars, prepaid forwards, and tax-loss harvesting can work together as part of a real plan.

No fluff. No hype. Just a better framework for making a high-stakes decision.

Enter your email and the guide will go straight to your inbox. No spam. Just the playbook — and a few follow-up notes to help you make sense of it.

Subject: The tax bill is not the only risk

Paul, one of the most common patterns in concentrated stock situations is that the tax bill becomes so intimidating that it turns into the only thing people can see.

That makes sense. Nobody wants to trigger a large gain unnecessarily. But doing nothing has a cost too, especially when one position has become a meaningful part of your balance sheet.

The real question usually is not “Should I sell everything right now?” It is “How do I reduce risk in a way that is thoughtful, tax-aware, and realistic for my situation?”

That is the reason the playbook matters. The answer is often not one move. It is a sequence.

One more useful sample

A simple nurture sequence you could send after the guide

If you want the site to feel even more helpful, the follow-up does not have to be complicated. A short sequence that educates, clarifies, and invites a reply is enough to move someone from curiosity to confidence.

Suggested sequence

Five-email structure

  • Email 1: Deliver the guide and explain how to read it.
  • Email 2: Reframe the main risk and reduce overwhelm.
  • Email 3: Clarify one common misconception that causes people to wait.
  • Email 4: Walk through a realistic case-study-style example.
  • Email 5: Invite a low-pressure reply or second-opinion conversation.
Sample email 1

Subject: Here is the playbook

Thanks for requesting the guide. I put it together for people who know they should probably do something, but do not want to make a rushed decision that creates unnecessary taxes or regret.

My suggestion: do not try to read it all at once. Start with the sections that sound most like your situation, then come back to the strategy comparison later.

Over the next few days, I will send a few short notes to help make the guide more useful in the real world. No spam. Just context that should make the decisions feel less murky.

And if you ever want to sanity-check what you are seeing, you can just reply to this email.

— Paul

Recommended lead magnets

How to use what you already built as true lead magnets

The strongest version of your lead magnet system probably does not require building a pile of new content. It is mostly a packaging and sequencing exercise around the assets you already have.

Guide-first path

Keep each pillar guide as the anchor asset, but make the value exchange clearer. The opt-in should promise a useful download plus a short follow-up series that helps the reader apply what they just learned.

Calculator-first path

Add an email capture at the results stage for visitors who want their numbers saved, interpreted, or followed by a short educational series tied to that exact calculator.

Second-opinion path

Offer a low-pressure “second opinion” CTA after the guide or calculator result. This fits your existing tone because it feels helpful and calm rather than promotional.

Suggested nurture tracks

Two educational series that would amplify what is already on the site

You do not need an elaborate funnel to make this work. A short, thoughtful sequence in your voice would go a long way because the site already has enough substance to support it.

Series 1

The guide follow-up series

This sequence is for someone who downloaded one of the three guides and needs help moving from understanding to action.

  • Email 1: Deliver the guide and explain how to use it.
  • Email 2: The cost of waiting too long to address concentration risk.
  • Email 3: One planning misconception that causes people to stall.
  • Email 4: A short walk-through of the most relevant strategies.
  • Email 5: A case study that mirrors the reader’s situation.
  • Email 6: A low-pressure invitation to get a second opinion.
Series 2

The calculator follow-up series

This sequence is for someone who ran their numbers and is already showing higher intent. It should connect what they saw in the calculator to the broader planning process.

  • Email 1: Here is what your results are really telling you.
  • Email 2: Why tax pain and decision paralysis often go together.
  • Email 3: The sequence matters more than the individual tactic.
  • Email 4: The questions worth answering before making a move.
  • Email 5: A short invitation to review the numbers together.
Segmentation

A simple intake quiz could make your follow-up much smarter

One of the easiest upgrades would be a short “Which playbook fits you best?” quiz. It would not replace your current resources. It would simply help route people toward the most relevant guide, calculator, and follow-up sequence.

  • Question 1: Which situation sounds most like yours?
  • Question 2: Roughly how large is the position, portfolio, or expected exit?
  • Question 3: What is your likely timeline?
  • Question 4: What is the main thing stopping you from acting?
  • Question 5: Would a free second-opinion conversation be helpful?

Why this helps

  • It gives you clearer context before a call.
  • It makes follow-up more personal and relevant.
  • It helps separate casual browsers from higher-intent prospects.
  • It creates a better experience without changing your voice or brand.
DIY roadmap

A realistic way to do this yourself over 60 days

If you wanted to improve the system without turning it into a giant project, this is a clean sequence that builds on what already exists.

Weeks 1–2

Audit the current paths

Map every way a visitor can move through the site today: homepage, guide pages, calculators, case studies, resources, scheduler, and contact points. Decide where email capture should happen and where it should stay optional.

Weeks 2–3

Tighten the offers

Refine the opt-in language for the three guides and add a clear value statement to the calculators at the results stage. The goal is to make the next step feel useful, not pushy.

Weeks 3–5

Write two short nurture series

Start with one guide-based sequence and one calculator-based sequence. You can later split them into pillar-specific variants once the basic structure proves useful.

Weeks 5–6

Add simple segmentation

Use a short intake quiz or form logic to tag visitors by concentrated stock, business exit, or real estate exit. Keep it short and easy to complete.

Weeks 6–8

Connect to scheduling

Make sure the nurture sequences and calculator follow-up naturally point toward a low-pressure second-opinion call for people who want help applying what they learned.

Priority task list

If only three things happen next, make them these

1. Add smarter capture

Make the guides and calculators work as clear opt-in points with relevant follow-up tied to what the visitor actually used.

2. Build nurture in your voice

Create short educational sequences that sound like you and deepen trust after someone downloads or calculates.

3. Create a second-opinion bridge

Give readers a natural next step when they are ready to move from self-education into a personalized conversation.
